Space Outlaws

A is a 3D beat 'em up inspired by games like Yakuza and God Hand, where the player assumes the role of a bounty hunter who selects missions from a central hub and then ventures into different mission levels while also managing their finances.

I initially started this project to experiment with various types of interactable environmental objects and weapons in a fast-paced combat setting. During the prototyping phase, I became deeply invested in the concept and decided to turn it into a full-fledged combat-focused game, influenced by both classic and modern beat 'em ups.

The core mechanics are fully playable and provide a solid foundation for future development.

Team Size: 2 people
Development Time: 4 months
Technology: Unreal / C++ / Blueprints

All of the following systems are designed to be easily expandable and editable via Blueprints, allowing VFX and SFX to be easily changed or added, and enabling assets such as animations and actor meshes to be easily swapped when needed.

Combat system

  • Player-customizable combo system, allowing players to tailor their attack sequences.
  • AI-driven attack selection supports varied enemy designs, each with unique movesets, ensuring diverse combat encounters. Enemy characters are designed with flexible properties, making it easy to adjust power levels and introduce variety through Blueprint editing.
  • The system includes dynamic hit effects and states, such as juggling, staggering, stunning, and launching enemies, with directional hit reactions.
  • The combat mechanics offer an enemy grab system, a finisher system for cinematic takedowns, and a range of special attacks to enhance gameplay depth.

The moveset customization UI allows players to preview their moves and view detailed stats for each one.

Throwable Environment Objects and Weapons

  • Weapons with unique movesets for both player and AI driven by data assets.
  • Various objects that can be thrown during combat to introduce environment interactivity. A blueprint template allows to easily expand the environment pickable/throwable objects.

The player picks up one of the environmental objects and throws it at the enemy. You can also see the box drop a healing item.

Level Management

  • Level loading system.
  • Persistent data handling to manage the flow between:
    • HUB level
    • Arena levels
    • Mission levels

UI System

Modular and scalable, developed primarily with Blueprints for faster iteration and design.

  • Main Menu and Pause Menu, including settings and control scheme screens.
  • Shops for consumables and upgrades.
  • Player attack moveset personalization.
  • Level selection.
